## 4. DESCRIPTION OF THE SERVICE

### 4.1 — Accountability Platform

RunMatch is a **fitness accountability platform** designed to help users build and maintain consistent running habits. The core Service includes:

- **Goal Setting**: Users set daily running distance objectives and completion deadlines
- **Activity Tracking**: Running activity is verified through Apple Health, Strava, or other approved fitness tracking integrations
- **Accountability Mechanics**: Users may voluntarily commit financial stakes to their goals. If a user fails to meet their objective by their stated deadline, committed stakes are transferred to a designated recipient chosen by the user
- **Community Features**: Users may connect with other runners for social support, motivation, and friendly competition

### 4.2 — Skill-Based Competitions

RunMatch offers optional skill-based fitness competitions ("Competitions") as an engagement feature within the broader accountability platform. Competitions operate as follows:

- Users may create or join Competitions with defined objectives (running distance, push-up counts, or combined)
- Competitions may include voluntary entry contributions from participants
- Competition outcomes are determined **solely** by participants' own physical athletic performance
- The participant who achieves the highest score based on their athletic performance receives the pooled entry contributions
- No element of chance, randomness, or luck determines Competition outcomes
- **Competitions are fundamentally skill-based**: the only way to receive a payout is by achieving a physical fitness goal through personal athletic effort

### 4.3 — Commitment Contracts

The voluntary financial stakes and competition entry contributions available through the Service are structured as **commitment contracts** — voluntary agreements in which a user pledges their own funds as a motivational tool tied to achieving a self-set goal. These are:

- **Voluntary**: No user is required to commit financial stakes or enter paid Competitions
- **Self-directed**: Users choose their own goals, deadlines, and stake amounts
- **Performance-based**: Outcomes are determined entirely by the user's own actions and athletic performance
- **Not gambling**: There is no element of chance. Results depend solely on whether the user completes their stated physical activity goal
