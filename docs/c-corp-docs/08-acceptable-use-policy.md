## 1. PURPOSE

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") sets forth the rules and guidelines for using the RunMatch platform, including the mobile application, website, and all related services (the "Service"). This AUP supplements our Terms of Service and is incorporated into those Terms by reference.

RunMatch is a fitness accountability and skill-based competition platform. The integrity of the platform depends on every user participating honestly and treating others with respect. This AUP exists to protect that integrity and ensure a fair, safe experience for all users.

## 2. GENERAL CONDUCT

You agree to use the Service only for lawful purposes and in accordance with these guidelines. You will:

- Treat other users with respect and sportsmanship
- Participate honestly in all activities, objectives, and Competitions
- Provide accurate and truthful information in your profile and account
- Comply with all applicable laws and regulations in your jurisdiction
- Report any suspected violations of this AUP to connect@live-eos.com

## 3. PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES

### 3.1 — Activity Fraud and Data Manipulation

The following are strictly prohibited:

- **Falsifying fitness data**: Manipulating GPS data, step counts, distance, heart rate, or any other fitness metric through software, hardware, or any other means
- **Ghost running**: Having another person perform physical activities (running, push-ups) on your behalf while using your account or device
- **Device manipulation**: Using treadmills, vehicles, bicycles, drones, or any non-running method to generate distance data fraudulently
- **App spoofing**: Using GPS spoofing software, mock location tools, or any application designed to generate false location or activity data
- **Data injection**: Writing false workout data to Apple Health, Strava, or any other connected fitness service
- **Pace manipulation**: Artificially generating activity data at speeds inconsistent with human running capability
- **Session manipulation**: Starting and stopping workout sessions to inflate distance or rep counts dishonestly

### 3.2 — Account Abuse

- **Multiple accounts**: Creating or maintaining more than one account per person
- **Account sharing**: Allowing another person to use your account or using another person's account
- **Account selling**: Selling, transferring, or trading accounts
- **Referral abuse**: Creating fake accounts or using deceptive practices to earn referral bonuses or invite rewards
- **Identity fraud**: Providing false identity information or impersonating another person

### 3.3 — Competition Manipulation

- **Collusion**: Coordinating with other participants to predetermine Competition outcomes
- **Sandbagging**: Intentionally underperforming to manipulate baseline scores or future matchmaking
- **Shill entries**: Entering Competitions with accounts you control to reduce competition or manipulate payout distributions
- **Harassment of competitors**: Intimidating, threatening, or harassing other Competition participants
- **Exploitation of bugs**: Using software bugs, glitches, or unintended features to gain an unfair advantage in Competitions

### 3.4 — Financial Abuse

- **Chargebacks**: Filing fraudulent chargebacks or payment disputes for legitimate transactions
- **Money laundering**: Using the Service to process, transfer, or conceal funds from unlawful activities
- **Structuring**: Making deposits or withdrawals in patterns designed to evade reporting requirements
- **Fraud**: Using stolen payment methods, unauthorized accounts, or deceptive practices to deposit or withdraw funds

### 3.5 — Harmful Content and Behavior

- **Harassment**: Bullying, threatening, intimidating, or stalking other users
- **Hate speech**: Content or conduct that promotes violence or hatred against individuals or groups based on race, ethnicity, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or other protected characteristics
- **Offensive content**: Profile names, images, Competition names, or other user-generated content that is obscene, defamatory, or inappropriate
- **Spam**: Sending unsolicited messages, invitations, or promotions to other users
- **Doxxing**: Sharing another user's personal information without their consent

### 3.6 — Platform Abuse

- **Reverse engineering**: Decompiling, disassembling, or reverse engineering any part of the Service
- **Scraping**: Using automated tools to extract data from the Service
- **Interference**: Attempting to disrupt, overload, or impair the Service's infrastructure
- **Circumvention**: Bypassing security measures, access controls, or usage limits
- **Unauthorized access**: Accessing accounts, systems, or data that you are not authorized to access

## 4. ENFORCEMENT

### 4.1 — Detection

RunMatch employs automated systems and manual review to detect violations of this AUP, including but not limited to:

- **Pace validation**: Automated rejection of activities below a 4:00/mile pace threshold
- **Source verification**: Checking the originating device and application of fitness data
- **Pattern analysis**: Monitoring for unusual activity patterns, GPS anomalies, and suspicious account behavior
- **User reports**: Investigating reports from other users

### 4.2 — Consequences

Violations of this AUP may result in one or more of the following actions, at RunMatch's sole discretion:

| Severity | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **Warning** | Written notice of the violation and required corrective action |
| **Temporary suspension** | Account access restricted for a defined period |
| **Competition disqualification** | Removal from active Competitions with forfeiture of entry contributions |
| **Balance forfeiture** | Forfeiture of account balance in cases of fraud or financial abuse |
| **Permanent ban** | Permanent termination of account with no right of reinstatement |
| **Legal action** | Referral to law enforcement or pursuit of civil remedies for fraud, financial abuse, or other serious violations |

### 4.3 — Appeals

If you believe an enforcement action was taken in error, you may submit an appeal to connect@live-eos.com within fourteen (14) days of the action. Appeals will be reviewed and a response provided within thirty (30) days. RunMatch's decision on appeals is final.

## 5. REPORTING VIOLATIONS

If you suspect another user is violating this AUP, please report it to:

**Email:** connect@live-eos.com

- A description of the suspected violation
- Any supporting evidence (screenshots, timestamps, etc.)

All reports are treated confidentially. RunMatch does not disclose the identity of reporters to the reported user.
