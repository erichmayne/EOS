# RESTRICTED STOCK PURCHASE AGREEMENT

**RUNMATCH, INC.**

---

This Restricted Stock Purchase Agreement (this "Agreement") is made and entered into as of __________, 2026 (the "Date of Grant"), by and between:

**RunMatch, Inc.**, a Delaware corporation (the "Company"), and

**Erich Mayne** (the "Purchaser").

---

## RECITALS

A. The Company has authorized the issuance and sale of Eight Million (8,000,000) shares of Common Stock, par value $0.0001 per share (the "Shares"), to the Purchaser.

B. The Purchaser desires to purchase the Shares on the terms and conditions set forth herein.

C. The Company desires to sell the Shares to the Purchaser on the terms and conditions set forth herein.

---

## AGREEMENT

In consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

## 1. PURCHASE AND SALE OF SHARES

### 1.1 — Purchase

Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, the Purchaser hereby purchases from the Company, and the Company hereby sells to the Purchaser, **Eight Million (8,000,000)** shares of Common Stock (the "Shares") at a purchase price of **$0.0001 per share**, for an aggregate purchase price of **Eight Hundred Dollars ($800.00)** (the "Purchase Price").

### 1.2 — Payment

The Purchase Price shall be paid in full at the time of execution of this Agreement by check, wire transfer, or other method acceptable to the Company.

### 1.3 — Issuance

Upon receipt of the Purchase Price, the Company shall issue the Shares to the Purchaser. The Shares may be evidenced by book entry or by a stock certificate, at the Company's discretion.

## 2. VESTING

### 2.1 — Vesting Schedule

The Shares shall vest in accordance with the following schedule:

- **Twenty-five percent (25%)** of the Shares (2,000,000 shares) shall vest on the first anniversary of the Date of Grant (the "Cliff Date").
- The remaining **seventy-five percent (75%)** of the Shares (6,000,000 shares) shall vest in equal monthly installments over the thirty-six (36) months following the Cliff Date, such that all Shares shall be fully vested on the fourth (4th) anniversary of the Date of Grant.

### 2.2 — Acceleration

In the event of a Change of Control (as defined below), **one hundred percent (100%)** of the then-unvested Shares shall immediately vest and become free of the Company's Repurchase Option.

**"Change of Control"** means: (a) the sale or disposition of all or substantially all of the Company's assets; (b) the consummation of a merger or consolidation in which the Company is not the surviving corporation or in which the stockholders of the Company immediately prior to such merger or consolidation do not own more than 50% of the combined voting power of the surviving entity; or (c) the acquisition by any person or group of beneficial ownership of more than 50% of the combined voting power of the Company's outstanding securities.

### 2.3 — Repurchase Option

In the event the Purchaser's status as a Service Provider (as defined below) is terminated for any reason, the Company shall have the right (but not the obligation) to repurchase any or all of the Purchaser's then-unvested Shares at the original Purchase Price per share (the "Repurchase Option"). The Company must exercise the Repurchase Option within ninety (90) days of the termination of the Purchaser's service.

**"Service Provider"** means the Purchaser's service to the Company as an officer, director, employee, or consultant.

## 3. TRANSFER RESTRICTIONS

### 3.1 — No Transfer

The Purchaser shall not sell, assign, transfer, pledge, hypothecate, or otherwise dispose of any unvested Shares or any interest therein without the prior written consent of the Company.

### 3.2 — Right of First Refusal

Before any vested Shares held by the Purchaser may be sold or transferred, the Purchaser shall give the Company written notice of the proposed transfer, including the identity of the proposed transferee, the number of shares proposed to be transferred, and the proposed price and terms. The Company shall have thirty (30) days from receipt of such notice to elect to purchase some or all of the Shares proposed to be transferred at the same price and on the same terms.

### 3.3 — Lock-Up Agreement

The Purchaser agrees that in connection with any underwritten public offering of the Company's securities, the Purchaser will not sell, make any short sale of, loan, grant any option for the purchase of, or otherwise dispose of any Shares for such period of time (not to exceed 180 days) as may be requested by the Company or the managing underwriter.

## 4. SECTION 83(b) ELECTION

### 4.1 — Election

The Purchaser understands that under Section 83 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended (the "Code"), the excess of the fair market value of the Shares on the date the Shares vest over the Purchase Price paid for such Shares will be reportable as ordinary income at the time of vesting. The Purchaser understands that he or she may elect to be taxed at the time the Shares are purchased rather than when the Shares vest by filing an election under Section 83(b) of the Code with the Internal Revenue Service within thirty (30) days after the Date of Grant.

### 4.2 — Acknowledgment

**THE PURCHASER ACKNOWLEDGES THAT IT IS THE PURCHASER'S SOLE RESPONSIBILITY TO FILE A TIMELY ELECTION UNDER SECTION 83(b), EVEN IF THE PURCHASER REQUESTS THE COMPANY OR ITS REPRESENTATIVES TO MAKE THIS FILING ON THE PURCHASER'S BEHALF. THE PURCHASER IS RELYING SOLELY ON THE PURCHASER'S OWN ADVISORS WITH RESPECT TO THE DECISION AS TO WHETHER OR NOT TO FILE AN 83(b) ELECTION.**

### 4.3 — Obligation to File

The Purchaser agrees to file an election under Section 83(b) of the Code within thirty (30) days of the Date of Grant and to provide a copy of such election to the Company.

## 5. TAX MATTERS

### 5.1 — Tax Withholding

The Purchaser acknowledges that the Company may be required to withhold taxes in connection with the purchase or vesting of the Shares. The Purchaser agrees to make arrangements satisfactory to the Company to satisfy any such withholding obligations.

### 5.2 — No Tax Advice

The Purchaser acknowledges that the Company has made no warranties or representations to the Purchaser with respect to the income tax consequences of the transactions contemplated by this Agreement, and the Purchaser is in no manner relying on the Company or its representatives for an assessment of such tax consequences.

## 6. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

### 6.1 — Purchaser Representations

The Purchaser represents and warrants to the Company that:

(a) The Purchaser is acquiring the Shares for investment for the Purchaser's own account and not with a view to, or for resale in connection with, any distribution thereof within the meaning of the Securities Act of 1933, as amended (the "Securities Act").

(b) The Purchaser has had an opportunity to ask questions and receive answers from the Company regarding the terms and conditions of the investment and the Company's business, properties, prospects, and financial condition.

(c) The Purchaser is an "accredited investor" as defined in Rule 501(a) of Regulation D under the Securities Act, or the Purchaser has such knowledge and experience in financial and business matters that the Purchaser is capable of evaluating the merits and risks of the investment.

(d) The Purchaser understands that the Shares have not been registered under the Securities Act and may not be sold or otherwise transferred unless they are registered or an exemption from registration is available.

### 7.4 — Successors and Assigns
