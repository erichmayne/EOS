### Full Description

**Build habits that actually stick.**

The EOS helps you stay accountable to your fitness goals through voluntary commitment contracts. Set your daily objective, optionally put your money where your mouth is, and complete your goal to keep your funds.

It's simple: succeed and keep your money. That's real accountability.

**How it works:**

1. **Set Your Goal** — Choose your daily objective (push-ups, runs, etc.)
2. **Set Your Stakes** — Optionally put money at risk to hold yourself accountable
3. **Designate a Recipient** — Choose who receives your committed stakes (friend, family, or EOS)
4. **Complete & Keep** — Hit your daily goal and your stakes stay in your balance

**Features:**

• **Live Counter** — AI-powered camera tracks your push-up reps in real-time
• **Daily Deadlines** — Set your own deadline with countdown timer
• **Voluntary Stakes** — Optional accountability with money you choose to commit
• **Strava Integration** — Automatically track running goals via Strava
• **Compete with Friends** — Create fitness competitions with leaderboards
• **Flexible Scheduling** — Daily or weekdays-only goals
• **Custom Recipients** — Invite friends or family as your accountability partners
• **Progress Tracking** — See your streaks and consistency

**Why commitment contracts work:**

Research shows that pre-commitment increases goal completion by up to 3x. When you've committed to your own goals, showing up becomes natural. The EOS harnesses this psychology to help you build lasting fitness habits.

No penalties. No punishments. Just you, your commitment, and real stakes.

Download The EOS today and commit to becoming your best self.

## App Review Notes

The EOS is a fitness accountability app that helps users build consistent habits through voluntary commitment contracts and friendly fitness competitions.

**IMPORTANT - This is NOT a penalty or gambling system. Three key points:**

1. **Users deposit their OWN money first, then voluntarily choose to put it at risk.** They are never "charged" or "penalized" — they pre-commit their own existing balance. The money is already theirs.

2. **The outcome is 100% within the user's control through physical effort.** This is not chance-based. If the user completes their push-ups or runs their miles, they keep every dollar. The user has full agency.

3. **Setting any financial stakes is completely optional.** The entire app works without money. Users can set goals, track push-ups, join competitions, and use every feature for free. Financial stakes are an opt-in accountability tool.

**Approved precedents with the identical model:**
- StickK (App ID: 334468932) — Approved 2012, still active, processed $50M+ in voluntary commitment contracts
- StepBet (App ID: 1056175729) — Approved, uses financial stakes on fitness goals, users bet on their own performance
- Beeminder — Approved, charges users who go off track on self-set goals

**Why this is not Guideline 3.2.2:**
- Users are not "charged when they do not meet targets" — they voluntarily pre-commit funds before setting any goal
- No automatic charges occur — all deposits are user-initiated through Stripe
- Users must explicitly acknowledge three checkboxes: stakes are voluntary, non-refundable, and they are 18+
- This follows established behavioral economics research from Yale University

**Core functionality:**
1. Users set personal daily fitness goals with customizable deadlines
2. Users can optionally set "Accountability Stakes" — money they voluntarily choose to put at risk to hold themselves accountable
3. Users designate a recipient (friend, family member, or EOS) who receives their committed stakes if they miss their goal
4. When users complete their daily goals, their full balance remains available for withdrawal
5. If users miss their goal, the stakes go to their designated recipient as per the commitment contract they agreed to

**Competitions Feature (Guideline 5.3.2 Compliance):**
- Users can create or join fitness competitions with friends
- Competitions track daily objectives (push-ups, runs, or both) over a set duration
- Leaderboards show progress based on days completed or cumulative effort
- Users may optionally contribute a voluntary entry amount when the competition starts
- The participant with the highest score at the end receives the pooled contributions
- This is a skill-based fitness challenge — outcome is determined entirely by physical effort, not chance
- All contributions are voluntary and clearly disclosed before joining
- Competitions without financial contributions are fully supported (bragging rights only)
- **Official contest rules are available in the app at all times** via the document icon in the Compete section toolbar — covering eligibility, scoring, fair play, refund policy, and disputes
- **The app explicitly states that Apple is not a sponsor** — this disclaimer appears in the contest rules, at the bottom of every competition leaderboard, and in the Terms of Service page

**Commitment Contract Framework:**
- Users explicitly acknowledge they are entering a voluntary commitment contract
- Users must check boxes confirming: stakes are voluntary, committed stakes are non-refundable, and they are 18+
- Users bear full responsibility for achieving their commitment
- This follows established commitment contract models (StickK.com has processed $50M+ in commitments)

**Full User Consent & Control:**
- Setting stakes is 100% optional — the app works without any financial commitment
- Users choose their own stakes amount
- Users set their own goals (objective type, target, deadline, schedule)
- Users select their designated recipient
- Users can withdraw their full available balance at any time
- No automatic charges — all deposits are user-initiated
- Users must acknowledge terms before setting stakes
- Competition contributions are clearly shown and must be agreed to before joining

**Key Legal Points:**
- This is a voluntary commitment tool based on behavioral economics
- Users are never "charged" or "penalized" — they voluntarily commit their own money
- Competitions are skill-based contests determined by physical fitness performance
- The same model as StickK (App ID: 334468932, approved 2012, still active)
- Based on research by behavioral economists including those at Yale

The camera feature uses on-device pose detection (Vision framework) to count push-ups in real-time. No images are stored or transmitted.

Strava integration uses official Strava API with OAuth for run tracking. Users connect their Strava account voluntarily.

**Account Deletion (Guideline 5.1.1v):**
- Account deletion is available in Profile > Account section > "Delete Account" button
- Users must enter their password to confirm deletion
- All user data is permanently deleted: account info, objectives, sessions, transaction history, competition data
- Deletion is immediate and cannot be undone
