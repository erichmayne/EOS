## App Review Notes

The EOS is a fitness accountability app that helps users build consistent push-up habits through voluntary commitment deposits.

**Core functionality:**
1. Users set personal daily push-up goals with customizable deadlines
2. Users can optionally deposit funds into their in-app wallet as a commitment mechanism
3. When users complete their daily goals, their full balance remains available for withdrawal
4. Users pre-select charities to support ‚Äî any funds not withdrawn are periodically donated to the user's chosen charities

**Full User Consent & Control:**
- Deposits are 100% optional ‚Äî the app works without any financial commitment
- Users choose their own commitment amount ($1, $5, $10, etc.)
- Users set their own goals (push-up count, deadline time, schedule)
- Users select which charities receive support from uncommitted funds
- Users can withdraw their full available balance
- No automatic charges to any payment method ‚Äî all deposits are user-initiated
- Users must explicitly confirm each deposit before funds are added

**Key points:**
- This is a voluntary commitment tool, not a penalty system
- Users are never charged ‚Äî they voluntarily pre-fund their own accountability wallet
- Similar model to savings/commitment apps (Qapital, Stickk, Beeminder) that have operated on the App Store for years
- The commitment mechanism is based on behavioral economics research showing pre-commitment improves goal achievement

The camera feature uses on-device pose detection (Vision framework) to count push-ups in real-time. No images are stored or transmitted.
