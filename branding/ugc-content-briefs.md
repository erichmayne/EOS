# EOS UGC Content Briefs

## Video 1: "Morning Accountability" (30-45 seconds)

### Concept
Creator talks about how EOS has kept them accountable for early morning runs — the real struggle of getting out of bed and how having stakes on the line changed everything.

### Setting & Wardrobe
- **Location:** Outdoors — sidewalk, park trail, or running path. 
- **Outfit:** Sporty and put-together —  Should look just finished or about to start a run.
- **Vibe:** Energetic, real, relatable. Not overly polished — 

### Script Outline

**Hook (first 2-3 seconds):**
"I used to set my alarm for 6 AM every single day... and snooze it every single day."

**Problem (5-8 seconds):**
"I told myself I'd start running in the mornings but I had zero accountability. 

**Solution (10-15 seconds):**
"Then I found this app called EOS. You set your fitness goals — mine is a 2-mile run every morning — and here's the thing: you put real money on the line. If I don't complete my run before my deadline, my (friend, family, BF-GF) gets the money i bet. once i set it, no backing out"

**Proof / Reaction (5-8 seconds):**
"It sounds intense but honestly? I haven't missed a morning in three weeks.

**CTA (3-5 seconds):**
"If you need that extra push to actually get out of bed, id give this a try. link's in bio. Trust me, it works."

## Video 2: "Winning Money Competing Against Friends" (30-45 seconds)

### Concept
Creator tells the story of starting a fitness competition with her friends on EOS — the group chat chaos, everyone suddenly becoming a morning person, and the satisfaction of winning the pot. Confident, playful, storytelling energy.

### Setting & Wardrobe
- **Location:** Outdoors — park path, trail, track, or open grass area. Good natural light, daytime.
- **Outfit:** Coordinated athletic set — matching leggings and sports bra or crop tank, running shoes, hair tied back. Should look cute but functional, like she actually works out in this.
- **Vibe:** Confident, playful, a little smug. She won and she knows it. Think roasting your friends at brunch, not trash talk at a gym.

### Script Outline

**Hook (first 2-3 seconds):**
"I just made money off my friends and honestly? They should be thanking me."

**Setup (5-8 seconds):**
"OK so me and my girls started this fitness competition on this app called EOS. Everyone threw in money, and whoever hits their goals the most days wins the whole pool."

**The Fun Part (10-15 seconds):**
"The group chat became unhinged. Like, girls who have not run since high school were suddenly posting their miles every morning. Someone missed a day and got absolutely destroyed. I was NOT about to lose money AND be the one who quit — so I was locked in. Run done, every single morning."

**The Win / Payoff (5-8 seconds):**
"Two weeks later the competition ended and the money hit my account. The app tracks your runs through Strava automatically so nobody can fake it — which is important because I don't trust any of them."

**CTA (3-5 seconds):**
"Start one with your friend group. Link in bio. Worst case you get in shape, best case you take their money."

### Key Talking Points (hit at least 3)
- Friend group competition with real money in the pool
- It made everyone suddenly motivated — social pressure actually works
- The group chat energy and roasting friends who slack
- App auto-tracks runs through Strava — no lying, no manual logging
- She won and it felt amazing

### Do's
- Be expressive — eye rolls, smirks, hand gestures, laughing at your own story
- Talk like you're telling your best friend — casual, not rehearsed
- Walk or stretch while talking — keep it moving and natural
- Say "my girls" / "my friend group" — makes it feel real and social
- Smile at the end — the vibe is fun, not cutthroat

### Don'ts
- Don't make it sound like gambling — it's a fitness challenge with your girls
- Don't over-explain how the app works — the story IS the sell
- Don't be actually mean about friends — playful teasing only
- Don't stand stiff in one spot — move, walk, gesture
