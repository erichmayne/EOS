# RunMatch — UGC Content Angles

## Creator Direction

Think "I just took my friends' money and all I had to do was run." That's the energy. We want content that makes people feel like they're missing out on something their friend group should be doing. Not a product demo — a story. You challenged someone, you showed up, and either you won or you're plotting your comeback. Make people want to download it just to prove something.

---

## Angles

### 1. The "I finally run now and I'm getting paid for it"
"I haven't run in two years. My friend challenged me on RunMatch — $50, one week, whoever runs more miles wins. I just collected and I haven't missed a morning since." Show the journey — from couch to payday. The alarm you actually get up for, opening RunMatch to check the leaderboard, the money hitting your balance.

### 2. The drag-your-friends-and-take-their-money
"I made my whole friend group download RunMatch. Now I take their money every week and they keep coming back for more." You started the competition on RunMatch, you're winning it. Show yourself sending the invite code, everyone joining the lobby, then the payout notification while they're all salty in the group chat.

### 3. The "get your man running and make him pay"
"My boyfriend said he could outrun me. Set up a competition on RunMatch. He couldn't. I'm $50 richer and he's finally in shape. Win-win." The challenge, the trash talk, pulling up RunMatch to show you're ahead. Show the money coming in and the look on his face.

### 4. The couple hustle
"We set up a RunMatch competition every week. Loser pays for date night." It's not even about the money — it's about bragging rights AND the money. Show both of you running, checking RunMatch standings, the playful rivalry, then the winner picking the restaurant.

### 5. The peer pressure pays
