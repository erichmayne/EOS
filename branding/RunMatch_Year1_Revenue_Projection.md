# RunMatch — Year 1 Revenue Projection

## What is RunMatch

RunMatch is a competitive running platform where users enter competitions to win cash — either against friends or in public matches. Users put money on the platform, compete in running challenges tracked via Strava, and winners take home cash prizes. RunMatch takes a 7% cut of every dollar on the platform.

---

## The Growth Engine

RunMatch has a built-in viral loop that most apps would kill for. Because it's a competition app, every user *needs* to invite friends to compete. This isn't an optional referral program — it's core to the product.

**The viral math:**
- Every user who downloads invites 4 friends to compete
- Those friends convert because there's cash on the line
- 25% of referred users bring 2 more people (public matches pull in strangers too)
- Effective multiplier: every 1 direct download generates ~6.5 total users

**Why this works:**
- Cash incentive = higher download conversion (8% vs industry avg 5%)
- Competition = built-in retention (12% monthly churn vs 20%+ for typical fitness apps)
- Social pressure = friends hold each other accountable to stay on the platform

## Three Scenarios

Lower content output (120 direct downloads/month), delayed ad spend (month 4), 3 friends per user instead of 4, higher churn at 18%, slower organic growth at 3%/month. This is the "nothing goes viral, steady grind" scenario.

- Active users by month 12: ~8,000
- Monthly cut by month 12: ~$3,600
- Year 1 total: ~$61,000

### Base Case — $151K Year 1

The model as described above. Consistent content, $1K/month ads from month 3, strong viral loop, 5% organic growth as the app gains traction.

- Active users by month 12: ~52,000
- Monthly cut by month 12: ~$29,000

### Bull Case — $362K Year 1

Higher ad spend ($1,500/month from month 2), public matches take off pulling in 5 friends per user, lower churn at 10%, 8% month-over-month organic growth as RunMatch starts trending in running communities.

- Active users by month 12: ~130,000+

- Year 1 total: ~$362,000

## Market Context

The running app space is booming:

- **Strava** adds 3 million new users per month with 180 million total registered users and $415 million in annual revenue
- **Runna** pulls roughly 400K downloads/month globally and ~$3.5M/month in revenue (acquired by Strava in April 2025)
- The fitness app market overall grew 24.5% in 2025 to $3.4 billion
- Gamification and cash-reward fitness apps (Sweatcoin, CashWalk) are among the fastest growing segments, with CashWalk seeing 42% year-over-year growth

RunMatch sits at the intersection of three proven models: fitness tracking (Strava), cash competitions (DraftKings/FanDuel), and social challenges (StepBet). No major player currently owns the "compete for cash by running" category.

## Key Risks and Assumptions

- **Regulatory**: Cash competition apps may face gambling regulations in some states/countries. Legal structure matters.
- **Churn**: 12% monthly churn assumes cash stakes keep people engaged. If the competition format gets stale, churn could spike.
- **Viral coefficient decay**: The 4-friend invite rate may slow as early adopters exhaust their running networks. Public matches are the hedge against this.
- **Average spend**: $20 every 2 months is modest. Power users in competition apps often spend significantly more, which could push revenue higher.
- **Fraud prevention**: Running verification via Strava/GPS is critical. Cheating would kill the platform fast.
- **Scaling assumptions**: The Runna comparison assumes RunMatch can achieve meaningful download volume. Reaching even 5% of Runna's monthly downloads requires significant marketing investment, strong app store presence, and sustained content output.
- **Market saturation**: As the active user base grows past 1M+, the viral coefficient may naturally decline as the addressable market of competitive runners narrows. Public matches mitigate this by opening the platform to casual runners.

## The Bottom Line

At a 7% take rate with a built-in viral competition loop, RunMatch is projected to generate between **$61K and $362K in year one revenue** from influencer-driven growth alone, with a base case of approximately **$151K**.

When benchmarked against Runna's download trajectory, the picture gets significantly larger. Runna reached 400K downloads/month in roughly 2 years with no viral loop. If RunMatch captures just 5–10% of that volume — achievable with the right marketing and partnerships — year one revenue jumps to **$15M–$30M** thanks to the 4x viral multiplier that Runna never had.

The key growth levers are:
1. **Ad spend** — even modest paid acquisition multiplies through the viral loop
2. **Public matches** — removes the ceiling on the viral coefficient by letting strangers compete
3. **Take rate optimization** — room to move from 7% toward 10% as the platform matures
4. **Partnerships** — race partnerships and Strava community integration (the same playbook Runna used)

By month 12 in the base case, the platform is generating roughly **$29K/month** in recurring revenue with a growing user base of **52,000+ active users** — setting up year two for significantly larger numbers as the compounding effect of the viral loop and organic growth accelerate.
